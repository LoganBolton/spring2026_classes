1. What is the difference between supervised training and one stage semi-supervised training?

Supervised learning is entirely reliant on pre-determined labels. One-stage semi-supervised training uses labeled and unlabeled data together in one stage by combining supervised and unsupervised losses.

2. What are the common loss functions used in Image Classification? Which of them does not involve the distributions of data, labels, and/or network predictions?

The common loss functions are cross entropy, contrastive loss, entropy minimization, Kullback-Leibler divergence, mean squared error, mutual information, and virtual adversarial training. Mean Squared Error does not rely on any distributions. 

3. What are Pseudo-Labels? Do you think doing Pseudo-Labels in supervised learning is a good idea?

Pseudo-Labels are labels generated by the model for unlabeled data. In cases where the orignial model is competent, this is reasonable. It is essentially just generating synthetic data to train a student model which is a standard approach in modern AI.

4. List the datasets used in the experiments.

They use CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, STL-10 and a subset of ImageNet.
